## What I learned
```
John M. Pollard is a British mathematician renowned for his contributions to number theory and cryptography. Born in 1941, he has developed several influential algorithms, particularly in the realm of integer factorization and discrete logarithms. In 1999, Pollard was honored with the RSA Award for Excellence in Mathematics.

In an RSA public key, you have two components:
    Modulus (n): A large integer, usually 2048 or 4096 bits long
    Exponent (e): Typically a small integer like 65537

The public key is essentially (n, e). The modulus is generated by multiplying two large prime numbers (p and q) and is critical to the security of RSA. The private key also relates to this modulus.
```
